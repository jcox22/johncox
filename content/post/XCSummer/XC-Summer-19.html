     Anybody who has ever run cross country knows the importance of summer training. Summer training is a great equalizer. By the end of track season, everyone kind of knows where they rank on the team. “Our top 5K guys are x, y, and z.” But who is the best runner at the beginning of cross country? The answer: “uhhh, depends on who trained the hardest this summer.”

 

     There aren’t a whole lot of secrets to summer training. There aren’t many workouts, there isn’t a ton of strength training, it mostly boils down to one thing: How many miles you run. That may sound quite boring or quite easy.
